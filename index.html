Hi, I'm Renee. I'm currently a graduate student at Duke University focusing on health and tech policy. Prior to attending Duke, I worked in healthcare consulting for two years, where I developed SOPs, directive policies, and educational materials for hospital staff to support clinical operations and workforce training.

My broader research interests focus on the ethical and policy challenges surrounding emerging biomedical technologies and healthcare governance, particularly the growing role of artificial intelligence in clinical decision-making. Within this broader focus, my research examines reproductive health policy and how insurance design and regulatory frameworks shape access to in-vitro fertilization (IVF).

At Duke, I serve as a Teaching Assistant for Communicating Science Policy (Emily Edwards). Previously, I was a Teaching Assistant at the University of Pittsburgh for Physics II (Matteo Broccio), Biology I Lab (Katie L. Wagner), and Organic Chemistry I & II (Eugene Wagner; Jaclyn Powell).
