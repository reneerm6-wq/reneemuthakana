Curriculum Vitae

Hi, I'm Renee. I'm currently a student at Duke University focusing on bioscience and tech policy. Prior to attending Duke, I worked in healthcare consulting for two years, where I developed SOPs, directive policies, and educational materials for hospital staff to support clinical operations and workforce training.

My research examines reproductive health policy, particularly how insurance design and regulatory frameworks shape access to IVF across different health systems. More broadly, I am interested in the ethical and policy challenges posed by emerging biomedical technologies, including the growing role of AI in healthcare and how AI systems influence clinical decision-making, healthcare delivery, and regulatory governance.

I am the creator of the US State IVF Access Database, a dataset of IVF insurance coverage and regulatory policies across all 50 U.S. states. You can access the dataset here.

You can access the IVF Access Index Map, an interactive visualization of cross-state variation in IVF access and insurance policy, here.

At Duke, I serve as a Teaching Assistant for Communicating Science Policy (Emily Edwards). Previously, I was a Teaching Assistant at the University of Pittsburgh for Physics II (Matteo Broccio), Biology I Lab (Katie L. Wagner), and Organic Chemistry I & II (Eugene Wagner; Jaclyn Powell).
